2.1 Electrical Units

Understanding the fundamental units used in electricity

A
Ampere
Measures electric current - the rate of flow of electric charge through a conductor.
Example: A typical household circuit can handle 13A, while a small LED might use 0.02A
V
Volt
Measures potential difference - the electrical pressure driving current through a circuit.
Example: UK mains voltage is 230V, a typical AA battery is 1.5V
Î©
Ohm
Measures electrical resistance - opposition to current flow.
Example: Light bulb filaments typically have several hundred ohms of resistance
W
Watt
Measures electrical power - rate of energy transfer.
Example: An electric kettle might be rated at 2000W, while an LED bulb might be 5W
C
Coulomb
Measures electric charge - quantity of electricity.
Example: A current of 1A flows when 1 coulomb of charge passes per second
J
Joule
Measures energy - work done by electrical current.
Example: A 60W bulb uses 60 joules of energy every second
s
Second
Measures time - duration of electrical processes.
Example: Used in calculating energy usage over time (kWh = kilowatt-hours)
