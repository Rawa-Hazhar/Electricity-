2.2 Electrical Safety in Domestic Appliances

Understanding essential protection methods in electrical devices

Double Insulation Demonstration

Primary Protection
I

Insulation

Non-conducting material covering electrical components

  • Prevents direct contact with live parts
  • Usually made of plastic or rubber
  • Must be regularly checked for damage
  • First line of defense against electric shock
Enhanced Safety
D

Double Insulation

Two layers of insulating material for extra protection

  • Eliminates need for earth wire
  • Common in portable appliances
  • Marked with square-within-square symbol
  • Provides backup protection
Critical Safety
E

Earthing

Safety connection to ground

  • Diverts dangerous currents away
  • Green/Yellow wire in plugs
  • Connected to metal cases
  • Prevents shock hazards
Current Protection
F

Fuses & Circuit Breakers

Overcurrent protection devices

  • Breaks circuit during faults
  • Different ratings for different appliances
  • Must be correctly rated
  • Prevents fire hazards
