2.1 Electrical Units

Understanding and using fundamental units in electricity

A Ampere

Current

Measures the rate of flow of electric charge through a conductor

Example: A typical LED uses 0.02A, while a kettle uses about 13A
V Volt

Potential Difference

Measures the electrical pressure driving current through a circuit

Example: UK mains voltage is 230V, batteries are often 1.5V or 9V
Ω Ohm

Resistance

Measures how much a component opposes the flow of electric current

Example: Common resistors range from 1Ω to several megaohms
W Watt

Power

Measures the rate at which electrical energy is transferred

Example: Light bulbs range from 5W (LED) to 100W (traditional)
C Coulomb

Charge

Measures the amount of electrical charge

Example: One ampere flows when one coulomb passes per second
J Joule

Energy

Measures the energy transferred in electrical processes

Example: A 60W bulb uses 60 joules of energy every second
s Second

Time

Measures the duration of electrical processes

Example: Used in calculating energy usage and charge flow
